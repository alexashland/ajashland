Introduction

A bit about me ...

I earned my PhD in English from the University of Iowa. My teaching and research interests include media and visual studies, critical memory, print culture, and material textuality. Currently, I am working on a book titled The Documentary Turn: U.S. Literature in the Age of Compromise, 1850–1877, in which I establish a prehistory and theory of documentary aesthetics as it emerged in the middle of the nineteenth century. My work has appeared in the The South Atlantic Review and Iowa Journal of Cultural Studies, as well as in edited collections, including The New Walt Whitman Studies and Ekphrasis in American Poetry.

    I worked as a Postdoctoral Research Assistant in the English Department at the University of Iowa, and as a contributing editor for The Walt Whitman Archive, where I transcribed, encoded, and annotated Whitman's correspondence. Additionally, I served as the project manager for the revised Whitman Image Gallery.

      I teach nineteenth- and twentieth-century U.S. literature and culture. In the past, I have taught courses in British and U.S. Literature, Writing and Composition, and tutored in the Writing Center.
